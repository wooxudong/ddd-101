What is Ubiquitous Language ?

Ubiquitous : existing or being everywhere at the same time, constantly encountered, widespread.

Ubiquitous Language : an common language for all based on domain models.

why do we need it ?

  1. Domain expert uses their jargon, whereas developers uses their own jargon
  2. Translation blunts the communication and thus pollutes the purpose and thus code.

How is it different from IPM ?

  1. When stakeholders / domain expert talks about implementations details.
  2. When the developer team is ignorant to know more about domain.

Layered Architecture

Every Layer's Responsibilities

  1. User Interface: interacts with users(human or computer) outside of the software.
  2. Application: coordinates the domain models in domain layer and delegates tasks to domain layer.
  3. Domain: reflects the concepts of the business.
  4. Infrastructure: provides generic technical implementations to support the higher layers, such as persistence.

What is Aggregate ?

an Aggregate is a cluster of associated objects that we treat as a unit for the purpose of data changes.

What for ?

  1. Minimize the complexity of associate between models
  2. Ensure some invariants are enforced across the objects

Invariants inside Student Aggregate?

  1. A student should not take more than 6 modules for each term
  2. A student should not take more than 40 modules for the entire study
